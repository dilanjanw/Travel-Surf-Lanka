Ancient heritage & scenic hikes

Sigiriya Lion Rock & Pidurangala Rock

Two of Sri Lanka’s most iconic viewpoints in one unforgettable adventure.

Pidurangala Rock

Panoramic view of Sigiriya

Just 10 minutes from Sigiriya Town, Pidurangala is known for its dramatic sunrise and sunset views.

Approx. 35-minute hike
  • Mix of stairs, flat paths, and rocky sections
  • Best for sunrise and sunset climbs
  • Comfortable footwear and water recommended
Sigiriya Lion Rock

UNESCO-listed rock fortress

Ancient ruins, frescoes, and garden remains make this one of the most famous cultural sites in Sri Lanka.

Nearly 1,200 steps • Approx. 55-minute climb
  • Spectacular summit views at sunrise or sunset
  • Rich history and restored heritage gardens
  • Wear supportive shoes and carry water
Comfortable clothing, proper footwear, and water bottles are recommended for both hikes.
Sacred city & garden escapes

Kandy

A cultural city surrounded by forests, spice gardens, and botanical beauty.

Temple of the Tooth Relic

The most sacred site in Kandy

Home to the Tooth Relic of Lord Buddha, with daily offerings of flowers and incense.

Poojas at 9.30 am and 6.30 pm
  • Traditional drumming and chanting
  • Modest dress required
  • Best visited with respectful timing
Udawattakele Forest Reserve

Quiet rainforest trek

Perfect for a peaceful walk among birds, monkeys, deer, and other wildlife.

1 km to 1.5 km trek
  • Wear comfortable shoes and clothes
  • Good option for gentle nature walks
National Spice Garden

Learn about Sri Lanka’s spices

See how famous spices are grown and processed, with products available to purchase.

  • Spices, herbal remedies, and cosmetics for sale
  • Free herbal drink offered to visitors
Peradeniya Botanical Garden

Large scenic garden

Known for orchids, medicinal plants, and palm-lined avenues ideal for relaxed walks.

  • Electric car hire available
  • Great place for a packed meal on the lawns
Wildlife & safari experiences

Picturesque National Parks in Sri Lanka

From leopard territory to elephant gatherings, these parks are some of the best safari destinations on the island.

Yala

Leopards and big game

One of the highest leopard populations in the world, with strong chances of seeing elephants, deer, wild buffalo, boar, sloth bears, monkeys, and many birds.

  • February and March are the best months for leopard sightings
  • Peacocks are especially photogenic here
  • Ideal for seeing elephants and leopards in the wild
Wilpattu

Largest and oldest park

Known for leopards, rich vegetation, and around 31 species of mammals.

  • Elephants, sloth bears, leopards, and water buffalo can be seen
  • Famous for villus, or natural lakes
Minneriya

The Gathering

Home to the largest elephant gathering in Asia, often with more than 300 elephants near the tank.

  • Evening safaris offer the best chances
  • Rich birdlife, crocodiles, and spotted deer
Kaudulla

Elephant migration area

A dry evergreen forest that forms part of the Mahaweli river basin ecosystem.

  • Elephants frequently move between Kaudulla and Minneriya
  • Grasslands, wetlands, hills, and rich plant life
Udawalawe

Reliable elephant sightings

One of the most popular parks after Yala and a top choice for year-round safaris.

  • Large elephant population with almost guaranteed sightings
  • Also home to deer, crocodiles, and water buffalo
  • Open grasslands and fresh natural surroundings

Beaches – South Coast

Relaxed coastal stops for swimming, surfing, snorkelling, and sunset time.

Tangalle

Peaceful beach days

Wide bay, gentle atmosphere, and scenic sunset views.

  • Great for swimming and diving
  • Ideal for relaxed beach time
Hiriketiya

Hidden south coast gem

A laid-back bay where the sea meets thick greenery and jungle.

  • Perfect for surfing and sunbathing
  • Great for unwinding
Mirissa

Sun and whale watching

Popular for beach relaxation, whale watching in season, and easy surf sessions.

  • Coconut Tree Hill is a major photo spot
  • Suitable for beginner and intermediate surfers
Weligama

Best for surf beginners

Soft sandy beach with many surf schools and guides.

  • Excellent for learning surfing and swimming
  • Highly recommended for beginners
Unawatuna Beach

Resort beach with coral life

A popular beach for tourists and locals, with snorkelling and turtle nesting opportunities.

  • Good for surfing too
  • Turtles may come ashore at night
Hikkaduwa

Coral gardens and marine life

Known for underwater adventures, diving gear hire, and nearby attractions.

  • Explore the marine national park
  • See Seenigama Viharaya, Mitiyagoda moonstones, and Hikkaduwa Lake nearby
Beaches – East Coast

Trincomalee & Arugambay

Clear water, white beaches, surf breaks, and relaxed coastal days along Sri Lanka’s east coast.

Trincomalee

Harbour town and beach retreat

Trincomalee is one of Sri Lanka’s main beach destinations and has one of the world’s largest natural harbours.

  • Popular for water sports, fishing, sea angling, and whale watching
  • Major attractions include Fort Frederick, Thirukoneshvaram Kovil on Swami Rock, and the Kinniyai hot springs
  • Nilaveli and Pigeon Island are known for clear water, white beaches, coral snorkelling, diving, and bird watching
  • The beaches here are perfect for sunbathing and relaxation
Arugambay

World-famous surf destination

Arugambay is one of Sri Lanka’s top surfing destinations and hosts international surf competitions.

  • Offers waves for both beginners and professionals
  • Popular surf spots include Pottuvil Point, Whiskey Point, Lighthouse Point, Crocodile Rock, Peanut Farm, and Elephant Rock
  • The area is also known for seafood and local Sri Lankan cuisine
Ancient City of Anuradhapura

Rajarata & sacred heritage

Anuradhapura, known as “Rajarata” or “The King’s Country,” was the first capital of Sri Lanka and is one of the island’s 8 UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

Ancient city

Stupas, lakes, and archaeological ruins

Filled with ancient stupas, scenic lakes, and archaeological ruins, the city reflects Sri Lanka’s rich history and religious heritage.

Sri Maha Bodhi

Sacred tree and village atmosphere

The sacred Sri Maha Bodhi Tree, believed to be the world’s oldest authenticated tree, is one of the most venerated sites in the country.

  • Visitors can also enjoy refreshing rainwater tank baths under shady trees
  • Experience the warmth of local village life
Waterfalls in Sri Lanka

From Kandy to Ella

Mountain cascades, trekking spots, and scenic waterfall viewpoints across the hill country.

Kandy to Nuwara Eliya

Ramboda Falls, Twin Falls, Devon Falls, and St. Clair’s Falls

  • Ramboda Falls is located in a narrow valley between two mountains, with a short downhill hike and a drop of about 109 meters
  • Twin Falls means two waterfalls together; both falls drop from about 45 meters and the area is rich in greenery, endemic herbs, and biodiversity
  • Devon Falls is about 97 meters high and offers a beautiful cascade with scenic hillside views
  • St. Clair’s Falls is about 80 meters high and 50 meters wide, flows through green hills and tea estates, and is known as the “Little Niagara of Sri Lanka”
Nuwara Eliya

Lover’s Leap and Bomburu Ella Waterfall

  • Lover’s Leap is a scenic trekking spot and waterfall about 30 meters high, linked to a tragic love story between a prince and a village girl
  • The trail is not too difficult, but the rocks can be slippery, especially in the rainy season
  • Bomburu Ella is considered the widest waterfall in Sri Lanka and is described as one of the most beautiful
  • It is surrounded by lush green grassland and is not suitable for bathing or camping
Ella

Ravana Falls, Nil Diya Pokuna, Diyaluma, Upper Diyaluma, and Kurullangala

  • Ravana Falls is about 25 meters high, around 5 km from Ella, and connected to the Ramayana story; the area is easily accessible but can be slippery
  • Nil Diya Pokuna is an underground blue pond linked by two underwater pathways and needs a jungle walk with a guided trek for safety
  • Diyaluma Falls is the second highest waterfall in Sri Lanka and can be reached by a challenging trek from Ella or Wellawaya
  • Upper Diyaluma is reached by a downhill hike and is famous for its natural infinity pools; great for swimming and bathing in safer weather
  • Kurullangala is a trekking destination named for ancient bird frescoes, located about 4 km from Nil Diya Pokuna, with around a 9-hour trek
Riverston

Misty mountain views

Riverston has a windy, misty climate with beautiful views across the central hills.

Scenic views

Sera Ella, Bambarakiri Ella, and Thelgamu Oya

Enjoy beautiful views of Sera Ella and Bambarakiri Ella waterfalls, along with the scenic Thelgamu Oya.

Trek to the peak

Pitawala Pathana and Knuckles Range

Trekking to the peak via Pitawala Pathana offers stunning views of the central hills and Knuckles Range.

  • Wear hiking shoes and comfortable clothes for an easier hike
  • You can also experience authentic rural Sri Lankan village life at the base of the mountain
Dambulla royal cave temple

Dambulla Golden Cave Temple

A large isolated rock site with ancient frescoes, Buddhist paintings, and a remarkable cave temple complex.

Temple history

King Valagamba’s refuge turned rock temple

King Valagamba once took refuge here in the 1st century BC before turning it into a rock temple.

  • The cave ceilings are covered with colorful Buddhist paintings and ancient frescoes
  • The temple holds many Buddha statues, including a recumbent Buddha carved from living rock
  • A small drip fountain still flows continuously and is used for daily rituals
Visit tips

Steps, timing, and atmosphere

There are about 370 steps to the top, and the climb takes nearly one hour round trip.

  • The best times to visit are early morning or evening
  • Enjoy cool forest air, bird songs, and a beautiful sunset
River safari & mangrove scenery

Madu River Boat Safari

A calm lagoon experience with wildlife, islands, cinnamon culture, and mangrove scenery.

Boat safari highlights

Private lagoon experience

Visit an old temple, explore Cinnamon Island, and learn how cinnamon is harvested.

  • Rich wildlife and mangrove scenery
  • Calm lagoon views
  • Possible fish massage stop
Wildlife you may see

Nature all around the river

Crocodiles, water monitors, wild boars, snakes, and more can often be spotted.

  • Great for slow scenic travel
  • Useful as a half-day coastal experience
